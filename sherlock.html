fMRI Sherlock

understanding changes in functional connectivity due to changes in realistic stimuli

I’m currently working with Professor Han Liu in the Statistical Machine Learning Group and collaborating with Professor Uri Hassan in neuroscience to understand how interactions between different areas of the brain change as visual and audio stimuli change. A difficult challenge here is to learn the number of clusters in the brain at each scene of the tv show Sherlock. Another scientific question we’re trying to answer is to determine which regions of the brain are related to higher-level processing and control as opposed to specialized processes (like the lower levels of the visual system). To tackle both questions, I am using Single Linkage Clustering to identify clusters in intersubject covariance matrices and am developing a method of identifying “hub” nodes that link between clusters.

2016-present, Princeton University
