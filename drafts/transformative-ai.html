Transformative AI

May 2023

Transformative AI: The application of statistical, machine learning, or artificial intelligence tools that has a significant impact on either daily life, an industry/sector/good, or world affairs. Transformative AI prompts re-thinking either the statistical/ML/AI tools, or the role they play in their application. Transformative AI doesn’t have to be accurate, useful, or safe–it just has to be used to some effect.

Pros:

  • Focuses on the impact
  • Focuses on choices around model creation, release, and adoption. Doesn’t require a conversation about sentience.

Drawbacks:

  • lumps a lot of technology into the same bucket (potentially putting classifiers with generative models) which can obfuscate discussions.
    • People still mean different things by “transformative” which can stretch from AGI (still) to a logistic regression classifier applied to a social problem like predicting recidivism.
  • Side-steps the question of whether this transformation is good or bad
    • But also potentially a good thing because it doesn’t assume that the transformation will be bad.

Transformative AI focuses on the choices that people will have to make to create, release, and adopt new technologies. Not all impacts of AI are/will be bad and the specific details of how models are trained, what data they are trained on, and how they’re released (what is exposed + made available) can enhance, change, and diminish their impact.

Terms like AGI can obscure the plethora of decisions and control points that there really are. While researchers may keep those decisions front of mind, those can be lost when communicating with those who aren’t making those decisions every day.

The term AGI and the discussion around it is still extremely useful for disambiguating conversations and for communicating assumptions held, so I don’t think it should go away. Transformative AI is another term that can signal a set of concerns that aren’t the same as terms like AI Safety, AI Ethics, Ethical AI, AGI, Alignment, Fairness, etc.

Who’s already using this term:

Transformative AI is already used in the LessWrong/EA communities. On LessWrong and EA Forum], the bar for transformative is given as “at least as significant as the agricultural or industrial revolutions”1. I do not think technology needs to be that significant in order to be transformative. Additionally, was it clear when the agricultural or industrial revolutions occurred that they would be transformative on that scale and in that particular ways? I continue to believe that we all have a lot more agency than we expect, and that those revolutions occurred because of institutions, and systematic issues, yes, but also individual choices and policies made during those times.

Is that extremely broad? Yes. Does that include many developments that have already existed for many years like the ranking algorithms used in YouTube / Netflix / Amazon? Yes. We need to continue to adapt to these technologies, even those that have been around for a long time. In some cases, adaptation changes wholesale the way those technologies work because we’ve decided as a society that we prefer the alternative. We cannot go into anything with the premise that it must exist any particular way or must exist at all. Unexpected and unaccounted for situations arise continually, especially with developing technology.
