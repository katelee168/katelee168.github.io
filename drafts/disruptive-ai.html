Say, Disruptive AI

May 2023

→ Comment on this doc here

We should use the term disruptive AI when talking about current + future ML/AI tools.

Disruptive AI: statistics, machine learning (ML), or artificial intelligence (AI) that disrupts.

Disruptive AI does not have to be accurate, useful, or safe–it just has to be used to some effect.

Pros:

  • Focuses on the impact
  • Focuses on choices that AI creators can make such as around model creation, release, and adoption.
  • Side-steps the question of “what is sentience”. AI can be dangerous or helpful regardless of what we consider sentience is.
  • Does not assume that the disruption is good or bad

Drawbacks:

  • Lumps many different types of technology together (potentially putting classifiers with generative models) which can obfuscate discussions.
    • Logistic regression classifiers are “disruptive” when applied to a social problem like predicting recidivism, however they are a very different technology than generative AI models.

Disruptive AI focuses on the choices that people will have to make to create, release, and adopt new technologies. Not all impacts of AI are/will be bad and the specific details of how models are trained, what data they are trained on, and how they’re released (what is exposed + made available) can enhance, change, and diminish their impact.

Is that extremely broad? Yes. Does that include many developments that have already existed for many years like the ranking algorithms used in YouTube / Netflix / Amazon? Yes.

We need to continue to adapt to these technologies, even those that have been around for a long time. In some cases, adaptation changes wholesale the way those technologies work because we’ve decided as a society that we prefer the alternative. We cannot go into anything with the premise that it must exist any particular way or must exist at all. Unexpected and unaccounted for situations arise continually, especially with developing technology.

Terms like AGI can obscure the plethora of decisions and control points that there really are. While researchers may keep those decisions front of mind, those can be lost when communicating with those who aren’t making those decisions every day. I continue to believe that we all have a lot more agency than we expect, and that those revolutions occurred because of institutions, and systematic issues, yes, but also individual choices and policies made during those times. Exactly how AI will disrupt systems depends our actions, institutions, and social forces.

Other common terms are AI Safety, AI Ethics, Ethical AI, AGI, Alignment, Fairness, etc. Choosing to use any one of these terms is a choice to communicate a particular set of concerns and assumptions. These terms, and the term AGI, should not go away. Disruptive AI is simply another term that can signal a particular set of concerns, in this case, a focus on the impact of algorithms.
