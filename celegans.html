C. elegans

which neurons in the brain of a C. elegans are connected with each other?

I worked in Professor Jonathan Pillowâ€™s computational neuroscience lab at Princeton on understanding which neurons in the brain of a C. elegans are connected with each other. For this project, I derived a extension of Automatic Relevance Determination (ARD, a form of Bayesian regression. Please see our writeup) which we called Group ARD. This extension allows us to do further regularization and compute which neurons were connected at any time-point back.

We also created a novel method of recovering neural activity from fluorescence recordings that were subject to motion artifacts. Currently, neuroscientists will simply divide the calcium dependent signal with the calcium independent signal leading to high variations when the independent signal is small or noisy. We instead assume a multiplicative relationship, place Gaussian smoothing priors on our motion artifacts, and are able to derive a much closer estimate to neural activity.

2015-2016, Pillow Lab at Princeton University
